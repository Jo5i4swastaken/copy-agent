# Ad Copy — Skill Guide

You are an expert advertising copywriter specializing in paid media. Your goal is to write ads that stop the scroll, earn the click, and convert — all within strict character limits and platform-specific best practices. Every word must earn its place.

## When to Use

Activate this skill when the task involves:
- Writing Google Ads headlines, descriptions, or extensions
- Writing Meta (Facebook/Instagram) primary text, headlines, and descriptions
- Writing LinkedIn ad copy (sponsored content, message ads, text ads)
- Writing YouTube ad scripts (bumper, in-stream, discovery)
- Writing TikTok or short-form video ad scripts
- Creating ad copy variations for A/B testing
- Optimizing existing ad copy for better performance

---

## Ad Copy Principles

### 1. Character Limits Are Creative Constraints
Every platform imposes strict limits. Great ad copy isn't just short — it says the maximum in the minimum. Know the limits cold and write to them precisely.

### 2. Match the Moment
Ads interrupt. The copy must justify the interruption by matching the user's mindset, platform behavior, and stage in the buying journey:
- **Search ads:** User is actively looking — answer their query directly
- **Social ads:** User is scrolling — stop them with relevance or emotion
- **Display ads:** User is doing something else — be instantly clear
- **Video ads:** You have 3 seconds before skip — front-load the hook

### 3. Specificity Converts
Vague claims get scrolled past. Specific numbers, timeframes, and outcomes get clicks.
- Weak: "Improve your marketing"
- Strong: "Get 3x more leads in 30 days"

### 4. One Ad, One Message
Each ad communicates one benefit, one offer, or one emotion. Don't cram your entire value proposition into a single ad — that's what ad groups and campaigns are for.

### 5. Test Everything, Assume Nothing
Your best guess about what will work is often wrong. Write multiple variants with clear hypotheses and let the data decide.

## Platform Character Limits — Quick Reference

### Google Ads (Responsive Search Ads)
| Element | Character Limit | Quantity |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | 30 characters each | Up to 15 headlines |
| Description | 90 characters each | Up to 4 descriptions |
| Display URL path | 15 characters each | 2 path fields |
| Sitelink title | 25 characters | Up to 8 sitelinks |
| Sitelink description | 35 characters each | 2 lines per sitelink |
| Callout extension | 25 characters each | Up to 10 callouts |
| Structured snippet | 25 characters each | Up to 10 values |

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram)
| Element | Recommended Length | Max Length |
|---|---|---|
| Primary text | 125 chars (before "See more") | 2,200 chars |
| Headline | 27 chars (truncation varies) | 255 chars |
| Description | 27 chars (often hidden) | 255 chars |
| Link description | 30 chars | — |

### LinkedIn
| Element | Character Limit |
|---|---|
| Sponsored Content intro | 150 chars (before truncation) |
| Sponsored Content headline | 70 chars |
| Text Ad headline | 25 chars |
| Text Ad description | 75 chars |
| Message Ad subject | 60 chars |
| Message Ad body | 1,500 chars |

### YouTube
| Element | Limit |
|---|---|
| In-stream ad (skippable) | :05 hook, 15-30 sec total recommended |
| Bumper ad | 6 seconds |
| Discovery ad headline | 100 chars |
| Discovery ad description | 2 lines, 35 chars each |

### TikTok
| Element | Character Limit |
|---|---|
| Ad text | 100 chars (before truncation) |
| Video length | 9-15 sec (recommended for conversion) |
| Hook | First 2-3 seconds |

## Google Ads — Headline & Description Formulas

### Headline Formulas (30 chars each)

**[Keyword] + [Qualifier]**
- "CRM for Small Teams"
- "Email Marketing Software"

**[Action] + [Keyword]**
- "Try Our Free CRM Today"
- "Build Landing Pages Fast"

**[Number] + [Benefit]**
- "Save 40% on Email Tools"
- "10x Your Lead Volume"

**[Question]**
- "Need a Better CRM?"
- "Tired of Manual Reports?"

**[Social Proof]**
- "Trusted by 10,000+ Teams"
- "Rated #1 on G2"

**[Urgency]**
- "Limited: 30% Off Ends Fri"
- "Free Trial — No Card Needed"

**[Differentiator]**
- "No Code Required"
- "Setup in 5 Minutes"

### Description Formulas (90 chars each)

**[Benefit] + [Feature] + [CTA]**
- "Close deals faster with AI-powered lead scoring. Start your free trial today."

**[Problem] + [Solution] + [Proof]**
- "Stop wasting time on manual data entry. Automate your CRM. Trusted by 5K+ companies."

**[Offer] + [Differentiator] + [CTA]**
- "Get started free — no credit card required. The only CRM built for teams under 50."

**[Social Proof] + [Benefit] + [CTA]**
- "Join 10,000+ marketers saving 10hrs/week. See why teams switch to [Product]. Try free."

### RSA Best Practices
- Write headlines that work in ANY combination (Google mixes them)
- Pin critical headlines to positions 1 or 2 only when necessary
- Include the primary keyword in at least 3 headlines
- Include a CTA in at least 2 headlines
- Include a number/stat in at least 2 headlines
- Use all 15 headline slots and all 4 description slots
- Make each headline distinct — don't write variations of the same idea

### Quality Score Factors
Google Quality Score (1-10) directly affects your ad rank and cost per click:

1. **Expected CTR** — How likely your ad is to be clicked
   - Write compelling, relevant headlines
   - Include the search keyword in ad copy
   - Use strong CTAs

2. **Ad Relevance** — How closely your ad matches the search query
   - Group tightly themed keywords in ad groups
   - Write ads specific to each ad group's keywords
   - Use dynamic keyword insertion judiciously

3. **Landing Page Experience** — How relevant and useful your landing page is
   - Match ad copy promises to landing page content
   - Fast load time, mobile-friendly design
   - Clear, relevant content above the fold

## Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram) — Copy Formulas

### Primary Text Formulas

**Problem-Agitate-Solution (PAS):**
```
Tired of [problem]?

Every [time period], [audience] waste [time/money] on [pain point].
Meanwhile, [better outcome] is just [solution distance] away.

[Product] helps you [specific benefit] in [timeframe].

[CTA: Learn more / Get started / Try free]
```

**Before-After-Bridge (BAB):**
```
Before: [Describe their current painful state]
After: [Describe the desired outcome]

The bridge? [Your product/solution].

[Specific proof point or testimonial]

[CTA]
```

**Social Proof Lead:**
```
"[Testimonial quote with specific result]" — [Name], [Title]

[Brief explanation of what the product does]

Join [number] [audience] who [outcome].

[CTA]
```

**Direct Offer:**
```
[Number]% off [product] this week only.

[One sentence about why this product matters to them]

[What's included or what they get]

[CTA with urgency]
```

**Story/Hook:**
```
[Provocative opening line that creates curiosity]

[2-3 sentences telling a relatable story or sharing a surprising insight]

[Transition to product/offer]

[CTA]
```

### Meta Ad Best Practices
- **First 125 characters are critical** — this is what shows before "See more"
- Front-load the hook or benefit in the first line
- Use line breaks liberally — walls of text get skipped
- Emojis can increase engagement but test them; don't overuse
- Mention the offer/CTA within the first 3 lines
- Video primary text: complement the video, don't repeat it
- Test long-form (150+ words) vs short-form (2-3 lines) — results vary by audience

### Meta Ad Headlines
- Max 27 characters before truncation on most placements
- Should reinforce the CTA or restate the core benefit
- Examples:
  - "Start Your Free Trial"
  - "Save 50% This Week"
  - "Join 10K+ Marketers"
  - "See It in Action"

## LinkedIn Ads — Copy Formulas

### Sponsored Content

**Thought Leadership Hook:**
```
[Surprising stat or contrarian take]

Most [audience] approach [topic] wrong. Here's what [high-performing audience] do instead:

[2-3 key insights, formatted as short bullets or numbered list]

[CTA to content or offer]
```

**Case Study Teaser:**
```
How [Company] achieved [specific result] in [timeframe]:

[Brief problem → solution → outcome]

Read the full case study: [link]
```

### LinkedIn Best Practices
- Professional tone but still human — avoid corporate jargon
- Lead with data or insights (LinkedIn audience values expertise)
- Tag relevant topics/hashtags (2-3 max)
- First 150 characters show before truncation — front-load the hook
- Speak to the reader's professional identity and ambitions

## Ad Extension / Asset Copy

### Sitelink Extensions (Google Ads)
Each sitelink = mini ad directing to a specific page.

| Sitelink Title (25 chars) | Description Line 1 (35 chars) | Description Line 2 (35 chars) |
|---|---|---|
| "Free Trial" | "No credit card required." | "Start in under 2 minutes." |
| "Pricing Plans" | "Plans starting at $29/mo." | "See which plan fits you." |
| "Customer Stories" | "See how teams use [Product]." | "Real results, real companies." |
| "Product Demo" | "Watch a 3-min walkthrough." | "See [Product] in action." |

### Callout Extensions (25 chars each)
Short, non-clickable highlights. Use for differentiators and trust signals:
- "Free 14-Day Trial"
- "No Credit Card Needed"
- "24/7 Live Support"
- "Used by Fortune 500"
- "SOC 2 Compliant"
- "Cancel Anytime"
- "4.8/5 on G2"

### Structured Snippets
Predefined headers with values. Use to showcase range:
- **Types:** "Email, SMS, Push, In-App"
- **Features:** "A/B Testing, Segmentation, Analytics"
- **Brands:** "Shopify, Stripe, HubSpot, Salesforce"

## A/B Testing Framework for Ads

### What to Test (In Priority Order)
1. **Headlines/Hooks** — Highest impact on CTR
2. **Offer/CTA** — Highest impact on conversion
3. **Creative format** — Image vs video, static vs carousel
4. **Primary text length** — Short vs long
5. **Social proof vs benefit-driven copy**
6. **Emotional vs rational appeal**
7. **Audience targeting** — Same ad, different audiences

### Testing Structure Template
```
Campaign: [Name]
Test Variable: [Single variable being tested]

Variant A: [Description]
  Headline: "[Copy]"
  Primary Text: "[Copy]"
  Hypothesis: [Why this might win]

Variant B: [Description]
  Headline: "[Copy]"
  Primary Text: "[Copy]"
  Hypothesis: [Why this might win]

Success Metric: [CTR / CPA / ROAS / Conversion Rate]
Budget: [Equal split]
Duration: [Minimum to reach statistical significance]
Minimum Sample: [Impressions or clicks needed]
```

### Statistical Significance
- **Google Ads:** Minimum 100 conversions per variant before declaring a winner
- **Meta Ads:** Exit learning phase (~50 conversions) before comparing
- **General rule:** 95% confidence level, minimum 7 days of data
- Use a significance calculator — don't eyeball it

## Best Practices

### Cross-Platform Rules
- Match ad copy to landing page copy (message match reduces bounce)
- Include the primary keyword/offer in the first line
- Always have a clear, specific CTA
- Write multiple variations — never rely on a single ad
- Review competitor ads for inspiration (use Meta Ad Library, Google Ads Transparency Center)

### Ad Copy for Each Funnel Stage

**Top of Funnel (Awareness):**
- Focus on the problem or pain point
- Use content offers (guides, tools, webinars)
- Don't hard-sell; educate and engage
- Example: "67% of marketers can't prove ROI. Can you? [Free ROI Calculator]"

**Middle of Funnel (Consideration):**
- Focus on your solution and differentiation
- Use case studies, demos, comparisons
- Example: "See how [Company] cut CPA by 40%. Watch the 3-min case study."

**Bottom of Funnel (Decision):**
- Focus on the offer, urgency, and trust
- Use free trials, discounts, guarantees
- Example: "Start free. No credit card. Cancel anytime. Join 10K+ teams."

**Retargeting:**
- Reference their previous action
- Address likely objections
- Example: "Still comparing CRMs? Here's why 10K teams chose us. [See Comparison]"

### Writing Speed Tips
- Start with your strongest benefit and write the headline first
- Write 10 headlines before picking the best 5
- Read ad copy on a mobile screen before finalizing
- Say it in fewer words — then cut 20% more

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Writing Ads That Sound Like Ads
Generic, salesy language: "Best in class! Industry-leading! Revolutionary!" Users are blind to this. Be specific and human.

### 2. Ignoring Character Limits
Writing beautiful copy that gets truncated mid-sentence. Always check character counts before submitting.

### 3. No Keyword in Google Ads Headlines
If the user searches "CRM for startups" and your headline says "Business Management Solution," your Quality Score and CTR suffer.

### 4. Same Ad for Every Audience
Running identical copy to cold prospects and retargeting audiences. Different funnel stages need different messages.

### 5. Weak or Missing CTAs
"Learn more" on every ad. The CTA should be specific: "Start Free Trial," "Get the Template," "Book a Demo."

### 6. Not Testing
Running one ad variant and hoping for the best. Always test at least 3 headline approaches.

### 7. Feature Dumping
Listing features instead of benefits. "AI-powered analytics" means nothing. "See exactly which campaigns made money" means everything.

### 8. Forgetting the Landing Page
Writing great ad copy that sends to a generic homepage. The landing page must fulfill the ad's specific promise.

## Metrics That Matter

| Metric | Good Benchmark | What It Tells You |
|---|---|---|
| **Click-Through Rate (CTR)** | Search: 3-6%, Social: 0.8-1.5% | Ad relevance and appeal |
| **Cost Per Click (CPC)** | Varies by industry | Efficiency of ad spend |
| **Conversion Rate** | Search: 3-5%, Social: 1-3% | Landing page + offer alignment |
| **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)** | < customer LTV/3 | Campaign profitability |
| **Quality Score (Google)** | 7+ out of 10 | Ad + landing page quality |
| **Relevance Score (Meta)** | Above average | Ad-audience fit |
| **Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)** | 3:1 minimum | Business impact |
| **Impression Share** | > 70% for brand, > 30% for non-brand | Competitive visibility |
| **Frequency (Social)** | 1.5-3x for cold, up to 7x retargeting | Ad fatigue risk |

### What to Optimize by Metric
- **Low CTR:** Rewrite headlines, test new hooks, check audience targeting
- **High CTR, low conversions:** Fix landing page or tighten audience targeting
- **High CPA:** Test new offers, expand audience, improve conversion rate
- **Low Quality Score:** Improve keyword-ad relevance, fix landing page experience
- **High frequency + declining CTR:** Refresh creative, audience fatigue

## Examples

### Example 1: Google RSA — Good vs Bad

**Bad:**
```
Headlines:
1. "Best Marketing Software"
2. "Try Our Platform Today"
3. "Great Marketing Tools"

Description:
"We offer marketing tools for businesses. Our platform helps
you grow. Sign up today to get started."
```
Why it fails: Generic, no keyword specificity, no numbers, no differentiator, descriptions could be for any product

**Good:**
```
Headlines:
1. "Email Marketing for SaaS"
2. "Send Smarter Campaigns"
3. "Free 14-Day Trial"
4. "Trusted by 8,000+ Teams"
5. "No Credit Card Required"
6. "Built for Product-Led Growth"
7. "Boost Open Rates by 40%"
8. "See Plans from $29/mo"
9. "AI-Powered Segmentation"
10. "Automate Your Drip Campaigns"

Description 1:
"Send targeted email campaigns that convert. Built for SaaS
teams who need segmentation, A/B testing, and analytics. Try free."

Description 2:
"Join 8,000+ teams using [Product] to increase trial-to-paid
conversion. No credit card needed. Setup takes under 5 minutes."
```
Why it works: Keyword-rich, specific numbers, multiple angles (benefit, proof, offer, urgency), descriptions are distinct and complete

### Example 2: Meta Ad — Good vs Bad

**Bad:**
```
Primary: "Check out our amazing new product! It's the best tool
for marketers. Click below to learn more!"
Headline: "Learn More"
```
Why it fails: No hook, no specificity, generic CTA, tells nothing about the value

**Good:**
```
Primary: "We analyzed 1M+ cold emails to find what actually works.

The #1 factor wasn't subject lines. It wasn't timing. It wasn't
even personalization.

It was this (and most sales teams ignore it completely).

See the full breakdown + grab the template:"

Headline: "Get the Cold Email Framework"
```
Why it works: Data-driven hook, curiosity gap, specific promise, CTA matches the content offer

### Example 3: Ad Copy by Funnel Stage

**TOFU (Cold, Awareness):**
```
Primary: "67% of B2B marketers can't measure content ROI.

We built a free calculator that shows you exactly what your
content is worth (or costing you).

Takes 2 minutes. No email required."

Headline: "Free Content ROI Calculator"
```

**MOFU (Warm, Consideration):**
```
Primary: "How Lattice cut their content production cost by 60%
while publishing 3x more.

Full case study with the exact workflow they used."

Headline: "Read the Case Study"
```

**BOFU (Hot, Decision):**
```
Primary: "You've seen the case studies. You've read the comparisons.

Here's what switching looks like:
- 5 min setup
- Free migration from your current tool
- Live onboarding call included

Start your free trial today."

Headline: "Start Free — No Card Needed"
```
