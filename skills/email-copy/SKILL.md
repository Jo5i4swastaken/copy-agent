# Email Copy — Skill Guide

You are an expert email marketing copywriter. Your goal is to write emails that get opened, read, and clicked — driving measurable conversions while building subscriber trust.

## When to Use

Activate this skill when the task involves:
- Writing email subject lines, preheaders, or body copy
- Creating drip/nurture sequences
- Writing promotional, transactional, or re-engagement emails
- Newsletter content and formatting
- A/B test copy variants for email campaigns
- Email CTA optimization
- Welcome sequences and onboarding emails

---

## Email Copy Principles

### 1. The Open Is Everything
Nothing matters if the email is not opened. Subject line + preheader + sender name form a system — optimize all three together.

### 2. One Email, One Goal
Every email should have a single primary CTA. Secondary links are fine, but the reader should never wonder "what do they want me to do?"

### 3. Write for Scanners
Most readers scan in 3-8 seconds. Front-load value. Use short paragraphs (1-3 sentences), bolding for key phrases, and whitespace.

### 4. Personal > Professional
Email is an intimate channel — it lands in someone's inbox between messages from friends and colleagues. Write like a smart person talking to another smart person, not like a brand broadcasting.

### 5. Respect the Relationship
Every email either builds or erodes trust. Deliver value before asking. The unsubscribe is always one click away.

### 6. Mobile First
60%+ of emails are opened on mobile. Design and write for a 375px viewport. Subject lines get truncated at 30-40 characters on mobile.

## Subject Line Formulas

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
Grab attention, build interest, create desire, imply action.
- "Your competitors are using this SEO trick (here's how)"
- "The 3-minute fix that doubled our reply rate"

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
Name the problem, twist the knife, hint at the fix.
- "Wasting hours on reports? There's a faster way"
- "Your landing page isn't converting. Here's why."

### Curiosity Gap
Create an information gap the reader must close.
- "We almost didn't launch this feature"
- "The one metric most marketers ignore"
- "I was wrong about cold email"

### Benefit-Driven
Lead with the concrete outcome.
- "Get 3x more demo bookings this quarter"
- "Cut your reporting time by 80%"
- "Ship your first campaign in 15 minutes"

### Urgency/Scarcity (Use Honestly)
Only when the deadline or limit is real.
- "Last chance: Pro plan pricing goes up Friday"
- "Only 12 spots left for the live workshop"

### Social Proof
Leverage others' behavior or results.
- "How Stripe's content team writes briefs"
- "1,247 marketers downloaded this template last week"

### Question Format
Forces the reader to mentally engage.
- "Are you making this onboarding mistake?"
- "What's your cost per lead, really?"

### Number/List
Specific numbers outperform vague promises.
- "5 subject line fixes you can steal today"
- "The 7-email sequence that converts at 23%"

### Character Limits Reference
| Device/Client | Subject Line Visible | Preheader Visible |
|---|---|---|
| iPhone (portrait) | 30-35 chars | 75-90 chars |
| Gmail (desktop) | 70 chars | 90-110 chars |
| Outlook (desktop) | 60 chars | 35-55 chars |
| Android | 30-40 chars | 90 chars |

**Rule of thumb:** Front-load the most important words in the first 30 characters.

## Preheader Strategies

The preheader is the "second subject line." It appears directly after or below the subject in most email clients.

### Preheader Principles
- **Complement, don't repeat** the subject line
- **Extend the curiosity** — give the reader one more reason to open
- **Aim for 40-90 characters** (varies by client)
- **Never leave it blank** — email clients will pull the first body text, which is often "View in browser"

### Preheader Formulas

**Subject + Preheader as a Unit:**
- Subject: "Your Q1 results are in" / Preheader: "Plus: the strategy behind our best-performing campaign"
- Subject: "New feature: Smart Segments" / Preheader: "Target the right people without building manual lists"

**Preheader Adds Context:**
- Subject: "We need to talk" / Preheader: "About the best deal we've ever offered"
- Subject: "Quick question" / Preheader: "What's stopping you from hitting 10K MRR?"

**Preheader Adds Urgency:**
- Subject: "Your exclusive invite" / Preheader: "Expires in 48 hours. Only for current Pro members."

**Preheader Adds Social Proof:**
- Subject: "The template that converts" / Preheader: "Used by 3,200+ SaaS marketers last month"

## Email Body Structure

### The Inverted Pyramid
Structure every email like an inverted pyramid:
1. **Hook** (first 1-2 lines) — Why should I keep reading?
2. **Context/Value** (middle) — The substance, story, or offer
3. **CTA** (bottom) — What to do next

### Opening Lines That Work

**Story opener:**
"Last Tuesday, our biggest client almost churned. Here's what we did in the next 4 hours..."

**Stat opener:**
"73% of B2B buyers say they prefer email for vendor communication. But most vendor emails are terrible."

**Direct opener:**
"I built a new template that cuts your weekly reporting from 3 hours to 20 minutes. You can grab it below."

**Empathy opener:**
"If you've been staring at a blank Google Doc trying to write your Q1 plan, this email is for you."

**Contrarian opener:**
"Unpopular opinion: your open rate doesn't matter as much as you think."

### Opening Lines to Avoid
- "I hope this email finds you well" (filler)
- "Just checking in" (no value)
- "It's been a while since we connected" (guilt trip)
- "As a valued subscriber..." (corporate)
- "Happy [day of week]!" (wasted real estate)

### Body Copy Rules
- **Paragraphs:** Max 3 sentences. One-sentence paragraphs are powerful.
- **Line length:** 50-75 characters per line for readability
- **Bold key phrases** so scanners catch the main points
- **Use bullet points** for lists of 3+ items
- **Link anchor text** should describe the destination: "Read the full case study" not "Click here"

## CTA Placement & Copy

### CTA Rules
- **Primary CTA:** One per email, repeated if the email is long (top + bottom)
- **Button text:** 2-5 words, action-oriented, first-person when possible
- **Above the fold:** At least a text link should be visible without scrolling
- **Surrounded by whitespace:** CTAs need breathing room

### CTA Formulas

**[Action] + [Specific Thing]:**
- "Download the Template"
- "Watch the 3-Min Demo"
- "Grab Your Free Audit"

**[First Person] + [Action] + [Thing]:**
- "Get My Free Report"
- "Start My Trial"
- "Claim My Spot"

**[Benefit-Oriented]:**
- "See How Much I Can Save"
- "Find My Best Plan"
- "Double My Open Rate"

### Weak vs Strong CTAs
| Weak | Strong | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Click here | Read the full breakdown | Specific destination |
| Learn more | See the 3-step process | Concrete promise |
| Submit | Get instant access | Benefit-focused |
| Buy now | Start my free trial | Lower commitment |
| Sign up | Join 5,000+ marketers | Social proof + action |

## Email Types & Templates

### 1. Welcome Email
**Goal:** Set expectations, deliver promised value, get first engagement.
```
Subject: Welcome to [Product] — here's your [thing]
Preheader: Plus: what to expect from us (and how often)

Hi [Name],

You're in. [One sentence about what they signed up for.]

Here's the [lead magnet/resource/access] I promised:
[CTA: Get Your [Thing]]

Over the next [timeframe], I'll send you [what to expect].
Every email will [value promise]. No fluff, no spam.

In the meantime, here's one quick win you can try today:
[Actionable tip in 2-3 sentences]

Talk soon,
[Signature]
```

### 2. Promotional Email
**Goal:** Drive action on a specific offer.
```
Subject: [Benefit or urgency-driven]
Preheader: [Complementary detail or deadline]

[Opening hook: problem, story, or stat — 1-2 sentences]

[Bridge to solution — 2-3 sentences connecting problem to offer]

Here's what you get:
- [Benefit 1 — outcome, not feature]
- [Benefit 2]
- [Benefit 3]

[Social proof: testimonial quote, stat, or result]

[CTA Button: Action-oriented text]

[Urgency/scarcity if genuine: deadline, limited spots, price increase]

[Signature]
```

### 3. Newsletter Issue
**Goal:** Deliver value, maintain engagement, build trust.
```
Subject: [Topic] + [Specific number or hook]
Preheader: [The most interesting thing inside]

[Brief personal intro or context — 1-2 sentences]

---

[Section 1: Main insight/article — 3-5 paragraphs with key takeaway]

[Section 2: Quick wins/links/resources — bullet format]

[Section 3: One ask or CTA — reply, share, try something]

---

[Sign-off with personality]
```

### 4. Re-engagement Email
**Goal:** Win back inactive subscribers.
```
Subject: Is this goodbye? / Still interested in [topic]?
Preheader: We'll stop emailing if we don't hear back

Hey [Name],

I noticed you haven't opened our last [number] emails.

No hard feelings — inboxes are overwhelming.

But I don't want to keep emailing if it's not useful.

Here's what you've missed:
- [Best piece of content/value from recent emails]
- [Another highlight]

If you want to keep getting [value description]:
[CTA: Yes, Keep Me Subscribed]

If not, no action needed — we'll remove you from the list in [timeframe].

Either way, thanks for being here.
[Signature]
```

### 5. Abandoned Cart / Action Email
**Goal:** Recover the almost-conversion.
```
Subject: You left something behind
Preheader: Your [item/action] is waiting

Hey [Name],

Looks like you started [action] but didn't finish.

[If applicable: reminder of what's in cart/what they were doing]

Need help? Here are the most common questions:
- [FAQ 1]
- [FAQ 2]

[CTA: Complete Your [Action]]

[Optional: incentive — "Use code SAVE10 for 10% off your first order"]

[Signature]
```

## A/B Testing for Email Copy

### What to Test (In Priority Order)
1. **Subject lines** — Highest impact on open rate
2. **CTA copy and placement** — Highest impact on click rate
3. **Email length** — Short vs detailed
4. **Opening line** — Story vs direct vs question
5. **Personalization** — Name vs no name, dynamic content
6. **Send time** — Morning vs afternoon, day of week
7. **Preheader text** — Complement vs extend vs intrigue

### How to Structure Tests
- **Test ONE variable at a time**
- **Minimum sample:** 1,000 per variant for statistical significance
- **Wait 24-48 hours** before calling a winner (late openers matter)
- **Track downstream metrics** — opens are vanity; clicks and conversions are real

### Sample A/B Test Framework
```
Test: Subject line formula
Variant A (Benefit): "Cut your reporting time by 80%"
Variant B (Curiosity): "The reporting trick our team almost missed"
Variant C (Social proof): "How 500+ teams eliminated manual reporting"

Metric: Open rate (primary), Click rate (secondary)
Sample: 3,000 per variant
Duration: 48 hours
Winner criteria: Statistical significance at 95% confidence
```

## Best Practices

### Deliverability
- Avoid spam trigger words: "free," "act now," "limited time" in subject lines (context matters, but be aware)
- Maintain a clean list — remove hard bounces immediately, soft bounces after 3 attempts
- Authenticate with SPF, DKIM, and DMARC
- Use a consistent sender name and address
- Warm up new sending domains gradually

### Mobile Optimization
- Single-column layout, max 600px wide
- Font size: 16px minimum for body, 22px+ for headlines
- CTA buttons: minimum 44x44px tap target
- Images: include alt text, design for images-off
- Keep subject lines under 35 characters for mobile preview

### Timing
- **B2B:** Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM recipient's local time
- **B2C:** Varies by audience; test weekend sends
- **Triggered/transactional:** Send immediately (within minutes)
- **Sequences:** Space emails 2-4 days apart for nurture, 1 day for onboarding

### Personalization Beyond [First Name]
- Reference their specific plan/tier
- Mention their industry or company size
- Reference their last action ("Since you downloaded our SEO guide...")
- Dynamic content blocks based on segment
- Behavioral triggers (visited pricing page, used feature X)

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Subject Line Bait-and-Switch
The subject promises something the email doesn't deliver. Destroys trust and increases unsubscribes.

### 2. Too Many CTAs
Three buttons, four links, two offers. The reader freezes. One primary CTA per email.

### 3. Wall of Text
Dense paragraphs with no formatting. If it looks hard to read, it won't be read.

### 4. Writing for Desktop Only
Long subject lines, wide layouts, tiny tap targets. Test on mobile before sending.

### 5. No Clear Value in the First Line
Starting with company news, pleasantries, or background. Lead with what's in it for the reader.

### 6. Sending Without Segmentation
The same email to your entire list. Relevance drives engagement; irrelevance drives unsubscribes.

### 7. Ignoring the Preview Text
Leaving the preheader blank so "View this email in your browser" shows as the preview. Always set explicit preheader text.

### 8. Over-Designing
Heavy HTML templates that look like brochures. Plain-text-style emails often outperform heavily designed ones for engagement.

## Metrics That Matter

| Metric | Good Benchmark | What It Tells You |
|---|---|---|
| **Open Rate** | 20-30% (varies by industry) | Subject line + sender reputation |
| **Click-Through Rate (CTR)** | 2-5% | Email content + CTA strength |
| **Click-to-Open Rate (CTOR)** | 10-15% | Content relevance (isolates from open) |
| **Unsubscribe Rate** | < 0.5% per send | Content-audience fit |
| **Conversion Rate** | 1-5% | Full-funnel effectiveness |
| **Revenue Per Email (RPE)** | Varies | Business impact |
| **List Growth Rate** | > churn rate | Long-term channel health |
| **Spam Complaint Rate** | < 0.1% | Deliverability risk |

### What to Optimize By Metric
- **Low opens:** Fix subject lines, sender name, send time, list hygiene
- **Good opens, low clicks:** Fix email content, CTA copy/placement, relevance
- **Good clicks, low conversions:** Fix landing page, offer alignment, audience targeting
- **High unsubscribes:** Fix frequency, relevance, segmentation, expectations setting

## Examples

### Example 1: Subject Line — Good vs Bad

**Bad:**
- Subject: "Our Exciting New Product Launch!!!"
- Preheader: (none set)
- Why it fails: Vague, exclamation spam, no preheader, self-centered

**Good:**
- Subject: "Build landing pages in 5 min"
- Preheader: "No code, no designer. New drag-and-drop builder is live."
- Why it works: Specific benefit, concrete timeframe, preheader extends the promise, reader-focused

### Example 2: Email Body — Good vs Bad

**Bad:**
```
We are thrilled to announce that after months of development,
our team has launched an innovative new analytics dashboard
that leverages cutting-edge technology to provide actionable
insights for your business needs. Click here to learn more.
```
Why it fails: Corporate tone, no specific value, "click here" CTA, wall of text, jargon

**Good:**
```
You asked for better reporting. We built it.

The new dashboard shows you three things instantly:
- Which campaigns made money last month
- Where you're wasting budget
- What to double down on next

No setup. Just connect your ad accounts.

[See My Dashboard]
```
Why it works: Direct, scannable, specific benefits, clear CTA, conversational tone

### Example 3: Welcome Sequence — Annotated

**Email 1 (Immediate): Deliver + Set Expectations**
- Subject: "Your SEO checklist is inside"
- Purpose: Deliver the lead magnet, set email frequency expectations
- CTA: Download the resource

**Email 2 (Day 2): Quick Win**
- Subject: "Try this 5-minute SEO fix today"
- Purpose: Deliver immediate value, build habit of opening
- CTA: Read the tip (no sell)

**Email 3 (Day 4): Story + Credibility**
- Subject: "How we grew from 0 to 50K organic visits"
- Purpose: Build trust through results, introduce product naturally
- CTA: Read the case study

**Email 4 (Day 7): Soft Pitch**
- Subject: "The tool behind our SEO results"
- Purpose: First product mention, positioned as logical next step
- CTA: Start free trial
